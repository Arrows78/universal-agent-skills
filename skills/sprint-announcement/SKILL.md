# Sprint Announcement Writer

## Purpose

Turn sprint information into impactful messages that inspire action and alignment. Use the Golden Circle framework (Why-How-What) to lead with purpose and impact, not just deliverables.

## Golden Circle Framework

Start every message from the inside out:

1. **Why** — Why does this work matter? What problem are we solving? What belief drives us?
2. **How** — How are we approaching it differently? What makes our approach valuable?
3. **What** — What did we ship? What are the concrete deliverables? (Only after WHY and HOW are clear)

Avoid starting with "what" (the ticket list). The goal is to make the reader care before they see the details.

## Workflow

1. Identify the audience: squad, stakeholders, leadership, support, or go-to-market.
2. Determine the message type: kickoff, mid-sprint update, review, release, blocker, or carry-over.
3. Ask: what is the single most important thing this sprint achieves? Lead with that.
4. Build the Why first. Connect work to user value, business outcome, or team mission.
5. Then layer in the How if it adds context.
6. End with What only as supporting evidence.
7. Make asks, blockers, and next steps explicit.

## Tone

- Lead with impact, not process.
- Use active verbs that imply change: "enabling", "eliminating", "reducing", "empowering", "unlocking".
- Be specific about outcomes, not just activities.
- Write in the team's working language by default.
- Do not mention AI tools or add generated-by signatures.

See `references/examples.md` for concrete examples of each message type.

## Output modes

- **Kickoff**: Why + How + Scope (prioritized outcomes, not ticket count)
- **Review**: Why + Impact metrics + Shipped items + Next steps
- **Release**: Why + What changed + Who is affected + Rollout + Support
- **Blocker**: Why it matters + Decision needed + Owner + Deadline

## Quality gate

Before finalizing, check that:

- The first line answers "why does this matter?" — not "what did we do?"
- Every bullet implies a change for the user or the business.
- Metrics or impact are included when available.
- Blockers and asks are explicit with owners.
- The message fits the Golden Circle: Why first, How second, What last.
- The tone is stakeholder-ready: no ticket dumps, no jargon.
