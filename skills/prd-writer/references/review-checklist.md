# PRD review checklist

## Problem and evidence

- The problem is framed from the user's perspective.
- Evidence is included: research, analytics, support tickets, sales feedback, or strategy.
- Evidence is separated from assumptions.

## Scope and requirements

- Goals and non-goals are explicit.
- Requirements are testable.
- Priorities are clear.
- Edge cases and permissions are covered.

## Metrics and learning

- Success metrics define direction, target, source, and timeframe.
- Guardrail metrics are included when there is risk of harm.
- Analytics events support decisions, not only reporting.

## Execution readiness

- Dependencies and owners are visible.
- Risks have mitigations.
- Rollout plan is realistic.
- Open questions have owners and deadlines when possible.

## Communication

- Executive summary is concise.
- Language is understandable by non-technical stakeholders.
- Technical details do not obscure the product decision.
